### Post 11: The POP Framework
---
Every AI platform. One framework.

P - Persona: Who should AI act as?
O - Objective: What specific task should it accomplish?
P - Parameters: How should it deliver? (tone, length, format, constraints)

This works for legal research. Document drafting. Case analysis. Contract review.

It works in ChatGPT. In Claude. In Gemini. In custom GPTs.

Why? Because these aren't platform-specific tricks.
They're principles of clear communication.

When you give AI:
- A clear role (Persona: "You are a legal research assistant specializing in employment law")
- A specific goal (Objective: "Find cases addressing non-compete enforceability in California")
- Defined constraints (Parameters: "Focus on cases from last 5 years, cite to official reporters, flag conflicting precedents")

You get dramatically better outputs.

Example:
BAD: "Find cases about non-competes"
GOOD: "You are a legal research assistant specializing in employment law (P). Find California cases from the last 5 years addressing non-compete agreement enforceability (O). Cite to official reporters, summarize holdings, and flag any conflicting precedents (P)."

Same AI. Same prompt box. Completely different results.

The POP Framework works for legal professionals because it mirrors how we train law clerks.

#POPFramework #LegalAI #PromptEngineering

### Post 14: The Agentic Spectrum for Lawyers
---
Not every legal task needs an agent.

Here's the Agentic Spectrum for legal practice:

ASSISTANTS: Human-in-the-loop, conversational, real-time
- Best for: legal research, document drafting, case analysis
- Example: Custom GPT that knows your jurisdiction's case law

AUTOMATIONS: Trigger-based, workflow-oriented, background
- Best for: client intake, deadline tracking, status updates
- Example: New matter → Folder creation → Conflict check → Calendar setup

AGENTS: Autonomous, multi-step, tool-using
- Best for: complex research across multiple sources, comprehensive analysis
- Example: Deep research across case law, statutes, regulations, and secondary sources

Most lawyers jump to agents because they sound impressive.

But 80% of the time, a well-built assistant is enough.

Why?
- Legal work requires judgment at each step
- You WANT human-in-the-loop for most tasks
- Simpler tools are easier to validate and trust

Start simple. Add complexity only when needed.

The skill isn't using the fanciest tool.
The skill is choosing the right tool for the legal task.

#AgenticSpectrum #LegalAI #RightToolRightJob
