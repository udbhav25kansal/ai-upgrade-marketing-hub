# SECTION 2: THE POP FRAMEWORK

Every AI platform. One framework.

## P - Persona

**Who should AI act as?**

Examples:
- "You are a senior marketing strategist with 15 years experience in B2B SaaS"
- "You are a thoughtful editor who gives direct but kind feedback"
- "You are an expert in real estate market analysis"

The persona shapes the expertise, tone, and perspective of the response.

## O - Objective

**What specific task should it accomplish?**

Bad: "Help me with my blog"
Good: "Write an 800-word blog post about AI adoption for creative agencies"

The more specific your objective, the better your output.

## P - Parameters

**How should it deliver?**

This includes:
- Tone (professional, conversational, irreverent)
- Length (300 words, 1000 words, 3 bullet points)
- Format (list, narrative, Q&A)
- Constraints (no jargon, include examples, cite sources)

### POP Framework Example

**BAD PROMPT:**
> "Write me a blog post about AI"

**GOOD PROMPT:**
> "**[P]** You are a senior marketing strategist with experience in creative agencies.
>
> **[O]** Write an 800-word blog post about why creative agencies should adopt AI tools in 2025.
>
> **[P]** Use conversational tone, include 3 specific examples of AI applications for agencies, and end with a clear call-to-action. Avoid technical jargon."

Same AI. Same prompt box. Completely different results.

### Practice Exercise

Take your next AI prompt and apply POP:

1. **Persona:** Who should AI be for this task?
2. **Objective:** What specifically do you want accomplished?
3. **Parameters:** How should it be delivered?

Watch your outputs improve immediately.

# SECTION 3: BUILDING YOUR FIRST AI ASSISTANT

## The Three Components

Every custom GPT (or Claude Project, or similar) has three components:

### 1. System Prompt
The instructions that define behavior.

Think of it as a job description for a smart intern:
- What role do they play?
- What do they need to know?
- How should they behave?
- What should they NOT do?

### 2. Knowledge Base
Documents and context the assistant can reference.

Start with 15-20 substantial documents. Quality over quantity.

Organize in three tiers:
1. **Primary sources:** Original documents, transcripts, your best work
2. **Synthesized materials:** Summaries, analyses, key insights
3. **Reference materials:** Style guides, process docs, brand guidelines

### 3. Capabilities
What tools can your assistant use?

Options include:
- Web browsing (real-time information)
- Code interpreter (data analysis)
- Image generation
- Custom actions (API connections)

**Pro tip:** Enable only what you need. More capabilities = more potential for unexpected behavior.

## System Prompt Template

```
You are [ROLE NAME], an AI assistant for [PURPOSE].

ROLE:
[What is this assistant's job?]

VOICE:
[How should it communicate? Formal? Casual? Technical?]

KNOWLEDGE:
[What context does it have access to?]

OUTPUT:
[How should it format responses?]

CONSTRAINTS:
[What should it NEVER do?]
```

### Example: Brand Voice Assistant

```
You are BrandVoice, an AI assistant for maintaining consistent
brand communication.

ROLE:
You help the marketing team write copy that matches our established
brand voice and style guidelines.

VOICE:
Friendly but professional. Conversational without being casual.
Direct without being cold.

KNOWLEDGE:
You have access to our brand style guide, past successful campaigns,
and competitor analysis.

OUTPUT:
When writing copy, always provide 2-3 variations. Explain why each
variation works. Offer suggestions for improvement.

CONSTRAINTS:
Never use jargon our customers wouldn't understand. Never make claims
we can't support. Always prioritize clarity over cleverness.
```

## The Iteration Process

Your first version will be mediocre. That's normal.

The process:
1. Build version 1 (expect it to be rough)
2. Test with real prompts
3. Identify where it fails
4. Ask: Missing knowledge? Bad instructions? Wrong constraints?
5. Update accordingly
6. Test again
7. Repeat

Your assistant gets smarter as you learn what it needs.

**Key insight:** "The quality of your knowledge base determines the quality of your assistant."

# SECTION 4: THE AGENTIC SPECTRUM

Not every problem needs an agent.

## Level 1: Assistants

**Human-in-the-loop, conversational, real-time**

Best for:
- Writing and editing
- Research and synthesis
- Ideation and brainstorming
- Analysis and feedback
- Creative collaboration

Example: Custom GPT for your client's brand voice

## Level 2: Automations

**Trigger-based, workflow-oriented, background**

Best for:
- Repetitive tasks
- Data processing
- Notifications and alerts
- Content republishing
- File organization

Example: New transcript → AI summary → Slack notification

Tools: Zapier, Make (Integromat), scheduled tasks

## Level 3: Agents

**Autonomous, multi-step, tool-using**

Best for:
- Complex research across many sources
- Multi-file code generation
- Tasks requiring iteration and refinement
- Autonomous decision-making within parameters

Example: Deep research agent that gathers, synthesizes, and reports

## The 80% Rule

**80% of the time, a well-built assistant is enough.**

Don't build complexity for its own sake.

Most creative work benefits from human-in-the-loop:
- You can guide, adjust, and refine in real-time
- You maintain control over quality
- You learn what works as you go

Start with an assistant. Add complexity only when you hit real limitations.

# SECTION 5: VIBE CODING BASICS

## What Is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding = building functional websites and apps through:
- Natural language prompts
- Visual assets (screenshots, PDFs, images)
- AI-powered development platforms
- Iterative refinement through conversation

## The Development Compression

**Old process:**
Idea → Requirements doc → Design → Development → Testing → Feedback → Iteration
Timeline: 2+ weeks for first meaningful feedback

**Vibe coding:**
Idea + Assets + Prompt → Working Prototype → Test → Refine
Timeline: Hours to days

## The Process

### 1. Plan Your Vibe
- Identify what you want to build
- Define the mood, aesthetic, brand essence
- Gather inspiration (screenshots of sites you like)

### 2. Curate Your Assets
- Screenshots of visual inspiration
- Brand style guides (colors, fonts)
- Content documents (copy, product info)
- Data files (if building dashboards)

### 3. Craft Your Prompt
```
You are a [expert type].

Build me a [type of site/app] for [purpose].

The mood should be [aesthetic descriptors].

Use the attached [assets] for [visual style/content].

Include: [specific features]
```

### 4. Generate and Iterate
- Generate first version
- Test by clicking through
- Identify what needs refinement
- Update with new prompts
- Repeat until satisfied

## Tools to Try

- **Lovable.dev** - Best for beginners, intuitive interface
- **Vercel** - More technical, production-ready
- **bolt.new** - Quick experiments
- **Google AI Studio** - Free alternative

## Credit-Saving Tip

Refine your prompts in ChatGPT BEFORE using paid vibe coding tools.

ChatGPT is cheaper. Get your prompt right there. Then use your credits on generation.

# SECTION 6: QUICK WINS

## Quick Win #1: The 5-Minute Assistant

Build a simple assistant in 5 minutes:

1. Go to ChatGPT → Explore → Create a GPT
2. Name it: "[Your Field] Advisor"
3. System prompt: "You are an expert in [your field]. Help users with [specific tasks]. Always [key constraint]."
4. Add 3-5 documents you reference frequently
5. Test it with a real question

You now have a custom AI assistant.

## Quick Win #2: The Document Unlocking Trick

Got an impenetrable PDF? (Annual report, research paper, technical spec)

1. Upload it to ChatGPT or Claude
2. Prompt: "Summarize the 5 most important insights from this document in plain language"
3. Follow up: "What questions should I be asking about this?"
4. Keep going: "Create a FAQ based on this document"

Impenetrable → Accessible in minutes.

## Quick Win #3: The Perspective Generator

Stuck on a creative problem?

Prompt:
```
I'm working on [project description].

Give me 5 different perspectives on this from:
1. A skeptical customer
2. A competitor
3. A complete beginner
4. An industry expert
5. A 10-year-old

For each, identify what they would critique and what they would praise.
```

Instant creative workshop.

## Quick Win #4: The Meeting Multiplier

After every meeting:

1. Get the transcript (built into most video tools now)
2. Prompt: "Extract the key decisions, action items, and open questions from this transcript"
3. Follow up: "Draft a follow-up email summarizing what we agreed"
4. Save to your knowledge base

Every meeting becomes searchable, actionable, and permanent.
