## POST 4: The POP Framework (Persona, Objective, Parameters)

Generic prompts = generic outputs.
Specific prompts = useful outputs.

This is the POP Framework:

**Persona:** Who should the AI be?
(Not "PR pro" but "Senior crisis communications specialist with experience in tech industry")

**Objective:** What exactly do you need?
(Not "write a pitch" but "draft the opening paragraph of a pitch to [specific reporter] about [specific angle]")

**Parameters:** What are the constraints?

## POST 5: Grounded vs. Ungrounded Tools (The Distinction That Matters)

Most AI education skips this critical concept.

**Ungrounded AI** (like vanilla ChatGPT):
- Draws from entire training data
- Can say anything
- Might be right, might be completely fabricated
- High hallucination risk

**Grounded AI** (like NotebookLM with your sources):
- Only accesses materials you provide
- Can only work with what you give it
- Dramatically reduced hallucination risk
- Research vs. gambling

For PR professionals, lawyers, healthcare workers‚Äîanyone who can't afford inaccuracy‚Äîthis distinction is everything.

When you use grounded tools for client work, you're not trusting AI's imagination.

You're trusting YOUR sources, with AI helping you synthesize them.

We teach the grounded research stack in AI Upgrade:

1. Perplexity for initial research (real-time, cited sources)
2. NotebookLM for grounded synthesis (your materials only)
3. ChatGPT for final output (structured writing)

Each tool for its strength. Reduced risk throughout.
