## The Distinction That Changes Everything

There are two types of AI tools:

**Ungrounded AI** - Draws from its entire training data. Can say anything. Might be right. Might be completely fabricated. No way to verify without external research.

Example: Asking ChatGPT "What were the key findings from [Company]'s Q3 earnings call?"

ChatGPT might give you an answer. It might even sound convincing. But unless it was trained on that specific data (unlikely), it's guessing. Confidently guessing.

**Grounded AI** - Only accesses materials you specifically provide. Can only work with what you give it. Dramatically reduced hallucination risk. Cites sources you can verify.

Example: Uploading the Q3 earnings call transcript to NotebookLM and asking "What were the key findings?"

NotebookLM can only reference what's in the transcript you uploaded. It cites specific passages. You can verify every claim.

**For PR professionals, this is the difference between research and gambling.**

## The Grounded Research Stack

Here's the workflow I teach in AI Upgrade for PR & Communications:

**Step 1: Perplexity for Initial Research**
- Real-time web access
- Cited sources (with links)
- Good for recent news, current events, trending topics
- Use for: Media landscape research, journalist background, current coverage

**Step 2: NotebookLM for Grounded Synthesis**
- Upload your materials (press releases, earnings calls, client docs, past coverage)
- AI can only reference what you provide
- Generates insights grounded in your sources
- Use for: Coverage recaps, message development, briefing materials

**Step 3: ChatGPT for Final Output**
- Take grounded insights from Step 2
- Use ChatGPT for structured writing
- Refine tone, format, polish
- Use for: Pitch writing, client deliverables, polished outputs

**Each tool for its strength. Reduced risk throughout.**

3. Use POP Framework for specific prompts:
   - **Persona:** "You're a senior PR strategist specializing in tech industry"
   - **Objective:** "Draft the opening paragraph of a pitch to [specific reporter] about [specific angle]"
   - **Parameters:** "Tone: conversational but professional. Length: 2-3 sentences. Must include [key message]. Avoid [common clichés]"
4. Generate grounded output
5. Critical review and refinement
6. Verify all facts against source materials
7. Polish in ChatGPT if needed
8. Ship with confidence

**This is systematic, not scattered.**

## The POP Framework (Persona, Objective, Parameters)

Generic prompts get generic outputs.

Specific prompts get useful outputs.

**The POP Framework:**

**Persona:** Who should the AI be?
- Not "PR pro" (too vague)
- But "Senior crisis communications specialist with experience in healthcare industry" (specific)

**Objective:** What exactly do you need?
- Not "write a pitch" (too broad)
- But "draft the opening paragraph of a pitch to Sarah Frier at Bloomberg about our patient care AI initiative, emphasizing data security" (precise)

**Parameters:** What are the constraints?
- Tone: Professional but approachable
- Length: 2-3 sentences max
- Must include: HIPAA compliance mention
- Must avoid: "Revolutionary," "game-changing," other hype words
- Format: Email-friendly, mobile-readable

**This level of specificity transforms AI outputs from generic to useful.**
